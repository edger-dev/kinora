# Content store is content-addressed

Every blob in `.kinora/store/` is named by the BLAKE3 hash of its bytes. The path is sharded on the first two hex characters:

```
.kinora/store/<ab>/<full-64-hex>
```

This buys three properties at once:

- **Global dedup** — identical content anywhere in the system writes one file. The store has no concept of "copies"; there is exactly one byte sequence per hash.
- **Integrity on read** — reading a blob re-hashes its content and compares against the path. A mismatch is a hard error, not a silent fallthrough.
- **Portability of identity** — you can talk about "kino `aabbccdd…`" independently of any branch, workspace, or repo. The hash is the kino's handle.

Blobs are pure content. System metadata — who wrote this, when, why — never lives inside the blob. That metadata is the ledger's job. Keeping the two separate means the same content composed from multiple sources collapses to one blob, and metadata can evolve without invalidating content hashes.
